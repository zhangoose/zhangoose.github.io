My next thing I need to figure out is CSS. Like actually figure out. Not just trial-and-error… As I have been guiltily doing for the most part. 

I will first start at w3schools (since just about everyone does that), then it’ll probably just be moving things around on test sites… AKA more trial and error. I mean, that’s a way to learn, right? But I think the key there is that instead of trial-error-forget, it should be trial-error-learn, so hopefully when you trial-error again, you’ll remember what happened last time! You know, have a brain and stuff. Okay, guiltily, I have not done that.

My general attitude towards CSS is “screw that”, “don’t feel like caring”, “ain’t nobody got time for that.” But I’m looking to change that! … or just improve upon my understanding of it so I’m not just splashing around chrome’s dev tools. 

You know, there really should be some kind of CSS-generator where you just move blocks around a website to where you want them, and you get CSS corresponding to those moves. I imagine there’d be a lot of complications of my idea of a CSS-generator (like a full-fledged one) due to the relative positioning. 

Anyways, back on topic. I’m tempted to read an actual book on CSS but I have a feeling CSS, like almost every programming language, is something you can only learn effectively by trying it out. So, for now, I will stick with w3.
