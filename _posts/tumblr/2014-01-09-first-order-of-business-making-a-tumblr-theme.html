So even though I really don’t like how Tumblr changed the way you post stuff (now it’s a drop-down kind of speech-bubble thing (wow, excuse the lack of terminology)), I decided to make my newly-purchased domain name to point to this Tumblr url (which I still don’t quite understand how that linking thing worked out, but it did, so, yeah), felt like a good place to start.

Only problem was trying to figure out how to make this Tumblr page look like a personal webpage. Then I stumbled upon iAlja’s blog and was like “oh hay, this could totes pass off as a tumblr and a personal webpage” so, being inspired by what she did with her site, I decided to take up this first order of business of making my own Tumblr theme! … and tackling my fear of CSS. I say fear, but it’s mostly just frustrations upon frustrations.

Anyways, so I dove in and mostly used none other than Tumblr’s Creating a custom HTML theme post.

Well, it kind of took a REALLY long time to wrap my head around because it’s been a while since I’ve done anything layout/theme-y (*cough* neopets *cough* gaiaonline *cough* therefore like 5-10 years ago). 

For some reason, I always thought meta tags in HTML were mostly used for SEO stuff (for page descriptions and titless). But apparently, for Tumblr, meta tags are used to tell Tumblr “ok I want whatever is in this meta tag to be customizable” which is actually pretty neat, another usage for meta tags, yay.
